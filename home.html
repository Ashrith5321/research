Simulating Human Leg Motion with Robotics

A research initiative at the Dynamic Systems and Control (DSC) Lab focused on biomechanical replication and intelligent charging through NVIDIA Isaac Sim.

Explore Our Work

About the Project

This research investigates the precise emulation of human leg motion using a robotic platform built within the Isaac Sim environment. We replicate joint dynamics on a 3-axis robotic table, simulating knee and ankle behavior, while integrating a dynamic wireless charging mechanism. The goal is to understand and optimize energy usage through biologically accurate motion replication.

Simulation Overview

  • Platform: NVIDIA Isaac Sim on Omniverse
  • Hardware Representation: XYZ Table with end-effector for shin-toe interaction
  • Control: Python scripted motions with closed-loop feedback
  • Goal: Human gait reproduction + wireless charging efficiency tracking
