Simulating Human Leg Motion with Robotics

A research initiative at the Dynamic Systems and Control (DSC) Lab focused on biomechanical replication and intelligent charging through NVIDIA Isaac Sim.

Explore Our Work

About the Project

Our research explores biologically accurate leg motion on a 6-DOF robotic arm, built first as a digital twin in NVIDIA Isaac Sim and later deployed on real hardware. A dynamic wireless-charging floor powers the system, letting us study energy use, gait efficiency, and safe autonomous locomotion.

Current Physical Platform

We are prototyping on a Flexiv-class 6-DOF arm mounted to a rigid XYZ table. The end-effector traces human-leg trajectories while a wooden panel beneath the arm hosts interchangeable charging pads. High-speed vision tracks foot placement; a ROS 2 bridge streams joint states between Isaac Sim and the real arm for hardware-in-the-loop testing.

Simulation Overview

  • Platform: NVIDIA Isaac Sim on Omniverse
  • Hardware Representation: 6DOF robotic arm with end-effector for shin-toe interaction
  • Control: Python scripted motions with closed-loop feedback
  • Goal: Human gait reproduction + wireless charging efficiency tracking
